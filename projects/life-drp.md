# Life DRP - Disaster Recovery Plan

Emergency preparedness plan for Julien (French, ~40), his wife (Thai, ~40), and their golden retriever, living in a three-story townhouse in Bangkok with a second property in Pranburi.

## Threat Priority

1. Natural disaster (flooding, earthquake)
2. Political instability / civil unrest
3. Pandemic
4. Regional military conflict
5. Economic collapse
6. Infrastructure failure

## Scenarios

This plan covers two timeframes:

- **72-hour crisis**: Earthquake, severe flooding onset, coup/unrest, infrastructure outage. Goal: shelter in place or evacuate to Pranburi.
- **2-week sustained crisis**: Prolonged flooding (2011-style), extended political instability, pandemic lockdown. Goal: self-sufficiency at home or at Pranburi property.

## Communication Plan

### If separated during an emergency

1. **Primary contact**: Call each other's mobile. If no answer, send LINE message.
2. **Rally point A (Bangkok)**: Home. If home is unsafe, meet at [agree on a specific landmark near your house — a temple, school, or gas station that's easy to find and unlikely to be damaged].

4. **Check-in schedule**: If comms are intermittent, try to contact each other at the top of every hour.
5. **Emergency contact abroad**: Julien's parents/sister in France as relay point — if neither can reach the other, call France to leave a status message.

## Evacuation Decision Framework

```
Is the home safe and habitable?
├── YES → Shelter in place (see Shelter-in-Place section)
└── NO → Is the road to Pranburi passable?
    ├── YES → Evacuate to Pranburi (see Evacuation section)
    └── NO → Go to Rally Point A and reassess
```

### When to evacuate vs. shelter in place

**Evacuate** if:
- Flooding is rising and approaching your area
- Structural damage to home from earthquake
- Active violence in your neighborhood
- Government orders evacuation of your district

**Shelter in place** if:
- Curfew declared (coup/unrest) but no immediate danger
- Pandemic lockdown
- Flooding elsewhere in Bangkok but not your area
- Earthquake occurred but home is structurally sound

## Evacuation Plan: Bangkok to Pranburi

### Vehicle choice

The BYD Seal is an EV (~460km rated range). This is a critical factor: **you cannot refuel an EV if the power grid is down**, and real-world range drops significantly with AC, load, and stop-and-go traffic.

| Scenario | Vehicle | Why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| Normal evacuation (both people + dog), charge >60% | BYD Seal | Space for dog, supplies, comfort, AC. 200km to Pranburi is well within range. |
| Charge too low / grid is down / uncertain about charging en route | Yamaha Tracer 9 GT + Honda ADV350 | Petrol bikes can refuel anywhere, even from jerry cans. Dog rides with wife in a taxi/neighbor's car, or shelter in place first. |
| Flooded roads (shallow) | Honda ADV350 or ADV150 | Higher ground clearance, can navigate around obstacles |
| Gridlocked traffic, solo scout | Yamaha Tracer 9 GT | Fast, agile, can filter through traffic to assess route |

### EV-specific rules

- **Keep the BYD Seal above 80% charge at all times** — this is your equivalent of "keep the tank above half." At 80% you have ~360km real-world range, enough for Pranburi with margin.
- **Stockpile petrol for the motorcycles**: Keep 10-20L of fuel stored safely at home. The bikes are your backup if the EV can't charge. The Tracer 9 GT has a 18L tank (~400km range), the ADV350 ~11.2L tank (~300km range), the ADV150 ~8L tank (~250km range). All three can easily reach Pranburi on a full tank.
- **Know the charging stops**: Identify DC fast chargers on both routes to Pranburi, but do not rely on them during a crisis — they need grid power too.
- **Grid-down scenario**: If power is out for an extended period, the EV becomes a liability. Motorcycles become primary transport. This reinforces why petrol reserves matter.
- **Silver lining**: A charged EV is a large battery. In a shelter-in-place scenario, you could potentially use the car to charge phones and power small devices (V2L — check if your BYD Seal model supports vehicle-to-load).

**Dog limitation**: If the car is not usable, evacuating with the golden retriever on motorcycles is extremely difficult. In this case, consider sheltering in place until roads clear, or finding temporary car transport (neighbors, taxi, rental).

### Routes

**Primary**: Highway 35 (Rama II) south to Highway 4. ~3 hours normally. Fastest but dangerous road with construction.

**Alternative**: Highway 338 (Borommaratchachonnani) to Highway 4 via Nakhon Pathom. ~125km longer but avoids the dangerous Rama II segment.

**Rule**: If evacuating during a crisis, use the alternative route. Rama II is already dangerous in normal conditions and will be worse during emergencies.

## Shelter-in-Place Plan

### 72-hour checklist

- Close and secure all doors/windows
- Fill bathtubs and large containers with water (backup supply)
- Charge all devices
- Monitor news via LINE, Thai PBS, FM91
- Avoid unnecessary travel
- Keep lights low during unrest situations

### 2-week sustainability

Your townhouse gives you vertical advantage during flooding (move supplies and living to upper floors). For a 2-week period you need:

- 120 liters of water (2 people + dog, ~4L/person/day + 2L/day for dog)
- Food for 2 people for 14 days (rice, canned goods, instant noodles, dried food)
- Dog food for 14 days (~6kg kibble for a golden retriever)
- Waste management plan (if plumbing fails, garbage bags + disinfectant)
- Entertainment/mental health (books, cards — morale matters in extended sheltering)
