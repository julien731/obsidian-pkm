# Ruay - Product Overview

## Vision

Ruay empowers everyone to build wealth — no financial degree required. By making every dollar visible and every decision intentional, Ruay transforms budgeting from a chore into a clear path forward. Whether you're living paycheck to paycheck or planning your first investment, Ruay meets you where you are and gives you the tools to take control, grow your savings, and build the financial future you deserve.

## Mission

Ruay's mission is to make financial clarity accessible to everyone. We give young adults, families, and anyone intentional about their future the power to see exactly where their money goes — and where it's going next. No jargon, no complexity, just a straightforward system that turns every paycheck into a plan and every plan into progress.

## What Is Ruay?

Ruay is a budgeting application that combines zero-dollar budgeting with the envelope system for modern digital banking. It helps people understand how much money they have and where it is.

## Core Problem

Most people don't know how much money they actually have available to spend. They see a balance in their checking account and think "I have $3,000" when in reality:

- $1,200 is for rent next week
- $400 is for utilities
- $200 is earmarked for groceries
- They actually have $1,200 of "available" money

Ruay solves this by making the invisible visible.

## Key Concepts

### Zero-Dollar Budgeting

Every dollar has a job. When income arrives, you allocate it to specific purposes until you have $0 left to assign. This doesn't mean you spend everything — it means every dollar is assigned somewhere, including savings goals.

### Envelope System

Money is virtually "placed" in envelopes for specific purposes. Just like the old-school cash envelope method, but digital. You can't spend more than what's in the envelope without consciously moving money from somewhere else.

### Physical Money vs Virtual Allocation

- **Accounts** = Where your money physically lives (checking, savings, credit cards)
- **Envelopes** = Where your money is virtually allocated (groceries, rent, vacation fund)

Your checking account might have $5,000, but that money is spoken for across multiple envelopes.

## Envelope Types

### 1. Spending Envelopes

Ongoing expenses without a target amount. Examples: Groceries, Utilities, Gas, Entertainment

### 2. Goal Envelopes

Saving toward a specific target amount. Examples: Emergency fund ($10,000 target), Vacation ($3,000 by July), New car down payment

### 3. Replacement Envelopes

Proactive saving for item replacement based on lifespan. The app calculates how much you need to save monthly to afford the replacement when the time comes.

Examples: Laptop (4-year lifespan, $2,000), Phone (3-year lifespan, $1,000), Car tires (3-year lifespan, $800)

### 4. Debt Envelopes

Tracking debt payoff progress. Examples: Credit card debt, Student loans, Car loan

## Expense Classification

Envelopes are classified by their nature to help identify budget optimization opportunities:

- **Fixed:** Must pay, amount predictable (Rent, Insurance, Loan payments)
- **Variable:** Must pay, amount varies (Utilities, Groceries, Fuel)
- **Discretionary:** Optional spending (Netflix, Dining out, Hobbies)

This enables "What can I cut?" — discretionary expenses are the first candidates when budgets are tight.

## Multi-Tenancy Model

### Households

- Households are the data isolation boundary
- Users can belong to multiple households (personal + family)
- Each household has its own accounts, envelopes, and budgets

### Privacy: Private by Default, Shared by Choice

- Accounts and envelopes are private to the user who creates them
- Users can explicitly share with household members
- Sharing has permission levels: View, Transact, Manage

Example: Partners might share "Utilities" but keep "Personal Fun Money" private.

### Household Roles

- **Owner:** Full access, billing management
- **Admin:** Full budget access, no billing
- **Member:** Create/edit transactions and envelopes
- **Viewer:** Read-only access

## Budget Periods

Budgets are recurring and tied to income periods: Weekly, Fortnightly, Monthly, or Custom.

Each income creates a new budget period. You allocate your paycheck to envelopes, and the app tracks spending against those allocations.

## Core Workflows

### 1. Initial Setup

1. Create household (or join existing)
2. Add accounts (checking, savings, credit cards)
3. Create envelopes for expenses and goals
4. Import existing transactions (optional)

### 2. Income Received

1. New budget period begins
2. Income appears as "To Be Assigned"
3. Allocate to envelopes until $0 remains
4. Copy allocations from last period (optional)

### 3. Daily Spending

1. Record transaction
2. Assign to envelope
3. Envelope balance decreases
4. See remaining available in each category

### 4. Month End

1. Review spending by envelope and category
2. Reconcile accounts with bank statements
3. Carry forward or zero out envelope balances
4. Start new budget period

## Target Users

- Individuals wanting to take control of their finances
- Couples/families managing shared expenses
- People in debt wanting a clear path out
- Savers wanting to track progress toward goals
- Anyone who's asked "Where did my money go?"

## What Makes Ruay Different

- **Unified Envelope/Goal Model:** Goals aren't separate — they're just envelopes with targets
- **Replacement Planning:** Proactively save for inevitable replacements
- **Expense Classification:** Quickly identify what you can cut
- **Privacy-First Sharing:** Share what you want, keep private what you don't
- **AI-Powered Insights:** Get answers to "Can I afford this?" and "What should I cut?"
