# Brand Naming Research

Thrifty was the working codename for a personal budgeting app combining zero-dollar budgeting with the envelope system. This document captures the naming exploration that led to selecting **Ruay** as the brand name.

## Decision: Ruay (รวย)

**Selected 2026-03-31.** Thai word meaning "rich." 4 letters, easy to pronounce in English, sounds like a clean brand name to international audiences. Personal connection for the founder (Bangkok-based).

**Registered domains (2026-03-31):**
- getruay.com (primary)
- ruay.money
- ruayapp.com

**Still available (not registered):**
- useruay.com
- ruaybudget.com
- ruaymoney.com

**Next steps:**
- [ ] Check social media handle availability (@ruay, @getruay, etc.)
- [ ] Check trademark conflicts
- [x] Update project documents to use "Ruay" instead of "Thrifty"

## Brand Context

- **Product**: Personal budgeting app — zero-dollar budgeting + envelope system
- **Audience**: Young adults, couples, families wanting financial clarity
- **Core idea**: Every dollar is visible and intentional; "make the invisible visible"
- **Tone**: Approachable, no jargon, empowering (not preachy)

## What Makes a Good Name for This Product

- Short and memorable
- Easy to say out loud and spell
- Hints at money/budgeting without being generic
- Feels modern, not corporate
- Works as an app name and domain

## Shortlist

| Name | Vibe | Strengths | Risks |
|------|------|-----------|-------|
| **Stash** | Friendly, familiar | Everyone knows what a stash is. Short (5 chars). Implies saving and storing — fits the envelope metaphor. | Very common word — likely taken in .com. Stash Invest is a known fintech brand. |
| **Ledgr** | Clean, modern | Nods to "ledger" — financial credibility. Dropped vowel feels tech-savvy. | Could feel cold/corporate. Spelling might confuse some users. |
| **Centsible** | Clever, warm | Wordplay (cents + sensible) is memorable and on-brand. Implies smart money decisions. | Pun names can feel gimmicky. Pronunciation is clear but spelling needs a second look. |
| **Koyn** | Bold, abstract | Very short (4 chars). Modernized "coin" feels fresh and brandable. International appeal. | Abstract — doesn't hint at budgeting specifically. Might get confused with crypto. |
| **DollarDuty** | Direct, purposeful | "Every dollar has a duty" is a built-in tagline. Clear connection to zero-dollar budgeting philosophy. | Longer (10 chars). Dollar-centric may not resonate internationally. |

### Round 2 Shortlist — wealth-building focus

Round 1 names felt too focused on the budgeting mechanic. The product isn't just about assigning dollars — it's about helping people build wealth. Budgeting is the starting point, not the destination.

| Name | Vibe | Strengths | Risks |
|------|------|-----------|-------|
| **Prosperly** | Aspirational, warm | Directly evokes prosperity. "-ly" suffix feels modern (like Shopify, Spotify pattern). Easy to spell and say. | Might sound generic. "Prosper" is a known P2P lending brand. |
| **Thrive** | Energetic, empowering | Universal appeal — everyone wants to thrive. Short (6 chars). Works across cultures. | Extremely common word. Thrive Global (Arianna Huffington) and many other brands use it. Domain nightmare. |
| **Headway** | Progressive, optimistic | Implies forward motion — "making headway" on your finances. Feels like a journey, not a chore. Favorite so far. | Headway is a book summary app with significant brand presence. 7 chars, solid length. |
| **Stackd** | Casual, builder energy | "Stacking money" is relatable slang. Dropped vowel feels tech/modern. Implies accumulation over time. | Trendy spelling may age. Could feel too casual for wealth-building positioning. |
| **Rizn** | Bold, abstract | "Risen" — your finances are rising. Very short (4 chars). Unique, no direct competitors. | Spelling is non-obvious. Might need explanation. Could feel crypto-adjacent. |

### Round 3 Shortlist — Ramp-inspired (short, real word, metaphorical)

Inspired by Ramp's brand: one real English word, short, not literally about money but metaphorically implies upward momentum. Confident and clean — no prefixes or suffixes needed.

| Name | Vibe | Strengths | Risks |
|------|------|-----------|-------|
| **Lever** | Empowering, smart | "Small actions, big results." Financial leverage. Works as verb and noun. 5 chars. Built-in tagline: "Lever your money." Implies intelligence — using a lever is about working smarter. | "Leverage" has finance connotations (debt leverage) that could cut both ways. Common word — domains may be tough. |
| **Tide** | Calm, confident | "A rising tide lifts all boats." Natural, steady growth. 4 chars — incredibly clean. Works across cultures. Implies something bigger than you carrying you forward. | Very common word. Tide is a detergent brand (different category, but high awareness). Could feel passive — tide happens to you vs. you taking action. |

### Round 4 — Fresh directions (French, Thai, mythology)

Explored three new angles: French words, Thai words, and mythology names for a brand rooted in clarity and wealth-building.

| Name | Origin | Vibe | Strengths | Risks |
|------|--------|------|-----------|-------|
| **Ruay** | Thai (รวย) = "rich" | Warm, insider, personal | 4 letters. Easy to say in English. Sounds like a clean brand name to non-Thai speakers. Personal connection (Bangkok-based founder). Strong domain availability — 5 confirmed .com options. | Associated with Thai lottery/gambling apps. Non-Thai speakers won't get the meaning without explanation (could be a feature or a bug). |
