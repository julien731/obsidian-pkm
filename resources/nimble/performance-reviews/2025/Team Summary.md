# 2025 360 Performance Reviews: Team Summary

This document captures common themes across the 2025 review cycle for the Product & Design team. 11 team members were reviewed by a total of ~50 reviewers (engineers, designers, PMs, recruiters, and external collaborators).

## Common Strengths

### Responsiveness and accessibility
Almost every PM was praised for being approachable, quick to respond, and easy to work with. This is a clear team-wide strength.
Mentioned for: [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Vu|Vu]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Aiden|Aiden]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Andrew|Andrew]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Clark|Clark]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Heri|Heri]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Jack|Jack]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Jui|Jui]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Tony|Tony]]

### Ownership and accountability
Team members consistently take responsibility for their work and go beyond their role when needed. Several PMs were noted for stepping in to help with client communication, QA, or troubleshooting even when it wasn't strictly their job.
Mentioned for: [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Aiden|Aiden]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Clark|Clark]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Got|Got]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Jack|Jack]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Julien V.|Julien V.]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Tony|Tony]]

### Clear communication with stakeholders
Multiple PMs are recognized for translating complex information into clear direction for engineering and design, and for managing client expectations professionally.
Mentioned for: [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Vu|Vu]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Clark|Clark]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Jack|Jack]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Julien V.|Julien V.]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Tony|Tony]]

### Proactive improvement mindset
Several team members actively look for ways to improve products, processes, and tools rather than just executing what's asked.
Mentioned for: [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Andrew|Andrew]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Heri|Heri]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Vu|Vu]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Jui|Jui]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Tony|Tony]]

## Common Areas for Improvement

### AI-generated ticket quality
The most widespread issue. Multiple PMs use AI to generate tickets, but the output often lacks verification — acceptance criteria don't fully make sense, edge cases aren't thought through, and context can be unclear or overly verbose. Tickets look polished on the surface but break down under scrutiny. This is a team-wide problem that needs a standard or review step.
Mentioned for: [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Heri|Heri]] (3 reviewers), [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Got|Got]] (1), [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Jui|Jui]] (2)

### Upfront requirement validation
Requirements gaps surfacing during development is a recurring theme. Constraints, edge cases, and assumptions not clarified before work begins, leading to rework, back-and-forth, and timeline pressure. Multiple reviewers across different PMs ask for better upfront alignment.
Mentioned for: [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Aiden|Aiden]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Clark|Clark]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Julien V.|Julien V.]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Jui|Jui]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Tony|Tony]]

### Written communication tone
Text-based communication (Slack, ticket comments) can read as blunt, commanding, or blaming — even when that's not the intent. This shows up independently for multiple team members and is a reminder that async written tone requires conscious effort.
Mentioned for: [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Aiden|Aiden]] (3 reviewers), [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Jui|Jui]] (1)

### Pushing back on clients
Several PMs are seen as too accommodating with client requests — accepting scope, timelines, or decisions without sufficient pushback. The more senior PMs (Vu, Julien V.) are praised for their firmness, while mid-level PMs need to develop this skill.
Mentioned for: [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Clark|Clark]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Got|Got]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Joseph|Joseph]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Tony|Tony]]

### Documentation and knowledge transfer
When projects change hands or new members join, the lack of up-to-date documentation creates friction. Backlogs, PRDs, and design specs are not consistently maintained. Ticket updates after discussions are sometimes forgotten.
Mentioned for: [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Got|Got]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Clark|Clark]], [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Vu|Vu]]

### Career development and growth structure
On the design side specifically, there's no clear leveling framework or career path. Designers don't know what's expected at each level or how to grow. This was raised by multiple reviewers for Andrew.
Mentioned for: [[resources/nimble/performance-reviews/2025/Andrew|Andrew]] (3 reviewers)
