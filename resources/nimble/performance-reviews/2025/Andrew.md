# 360 Review: Andrew (2025)

## Summary

**Strengths:**
- Proactive AI and tool adoption (3 reviewers) — drives AI experimentation, new workflows (Claude Code, Figma MCP), and encourages the team to adopt them
- Approachable and supportive leadership (4) — creates a transparent, safe environment for peer reviews, feedback, and raising concerns
- High design standards (3) — consistently upholds quality through structured reviews, clean UI, and strong UX focus
- Knowledge sharing and initiative (3) — proactively explores and shares new tools, best practices, and design thinking approaches
- Clear task assignment and direction (2) — provides detailed context, expectations, and reasoning behind work

**Areas for improvement:**
- Career path and leveling clarity (2) — no structured framework defining expectations, skills, and promotion criteria per designer level
- Team growth acceleration (1) — designers are good but their growth isn't visibly accelerating; more structured mentoring needed
- Strategic visibility (1) — could engage more in product strategy discussions and communicate high-level design vision
- Hiring alignment (1) — portfolio evaluation criteria are subjective; a scoring card or calibration sessions would improve sourcing efficiency

## What Could Be Better

### From Kade

Design hiring is very subjective, and at times there is a misalignment between what I anticipate he would like in a portfolio and his actual preferences. This makes it more challenging to pre-filter portfolios effectively and can slow down the funnel. A structured calibration session or clearer portfolio scoring card could significantly improve alignment. Also, I believe there is an opportunity for deeper alignment around the current designer talent market. Having more structured discussions on market availability, salary benchmarks, and typical portfolio standards would help us calibrate expectations and make sourcing more efficient.

### From Prajna Vira

Andrew has been an empathetic and strong role model for designers. Moving forward, it would be even more impactful if Andrew could demonstrate greater visibility and actively engage in product strategy discussions, helping shape and communicate the high-level design vision.

### From Got

As a lead, I expect more visible improvement from the rest of the team. Our designers are good, but their growth isn’t accelerating.

He should spend more time mentoring, giving structured feedback, and helping bridge the gap between his skill level and the other designers.

### From taus daengkul

We can try to expand the connection with the other designer community that will help us increase the knowledge and can learn from each other.

### From Thien

One opportunity I see for further strengthening the team is around career path clarity and leveling structure for designers. Currently, the expectations between different UX/UI levels are not very transparent, and there aren’t clearly defined objectives or competencies that designers can actively work toward in order to grow or be promoted.

Introducing a more structured leveling framework — outlining skills, ownership expectations, impact scope, and promotion criteria for each level — could provide clearer direction for individual growth. It would also help align performance expectations, support more objective evaluations, and increase motivation across the team.

Given his leadership and understanding of the team’s strengths, I believe he is well positioned to define and formalize this structure in a way that supports both individual development and long-term team scalability.

### From Laura

While Andrew is great at project-level support, we could benefit from more structured, long-term career development planning.
