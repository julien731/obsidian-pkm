# 360 Review: Julien V. (2025)

## Summary

**Strengths:**
- Strong ownership and high standards (3 reviewers) — sets a quality bar that others follow, takes full responsibility for the product he owns
- Supportive and collaborative (3) — trusted partner, empowers engineers, builds trust-based working environment
- Technical depth with product balance (2) — strong technical knowledge without over-indexing on either tech or user needs
- Efficient execution through ambiguity (2) — navigates chaos well, makes timely decisions that keep the team unblocked
- Clear, structured communication (2) — concise, to the point, serves as a model for the team

**Areas for improvement:**
- Requirement depth for complex features (1) — more detailed acceptance criteria and earlier edge case identification would help, especially for junior engineers
- Ownership delegation calibration (1) — gives ownership with good intentions, but sometimes engineers aren't ready for it; needs to read the audience
- Resilience with difficult clients (1) — strengths shine with engaged clients but challenging ones can demotivate; building resilience for tough days
- Sprint preparation (1) — tasks sometimes created during the sprint rather than prepared in the backlog beforehand

Regarding collaboration with the development team, he was willing to spend time on discussions and made timely decisions that prevented the team from being blocked. He built a trust-based working environment, which is reflected in his active participation in discussions and his respect for technical constraints. He aligned with engineering on the feasibility of complex features during the discussion stage, encouraged the team to propose improvements, and made decisions that moved the team forward.

## What Could Be Better

### From Huey

For complex features or epics, the development team would benefit from more clarity and depth in the requirements. Providing more detailed acceptance criteria and identifying edge cases earlier would help reduce ambiguity and minimize back-and-forth during development. This is especially important for junior engineers — if they follow the story’s AC without critical thinking, some edge cases might be missed.

As a team lead, I often take responsibility for clarifying unclear aspects of the requirements to help the team gain better insight into the assignment and ensure feature alignment across teams.

I understand that the GT mobile team does not publish the app to production, but it would be very helpful if he could share more user data, research insights, or success metrics with the team, client happiness...

Overall, he has had a very positive impact on the teams, not only GT but also the brand apps. I am happy to work with him on future projects (it’s sad that he is leaving GT for a new project).

On a personal note, he is a friendly and gentle teammate. I can talk with him about life topics, not just work.
