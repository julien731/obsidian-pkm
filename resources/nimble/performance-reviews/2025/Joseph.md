# 360 Review: Joseph (2025)

## Summary

**Strengths:**
- Strong communication and collaboration (2 reviewers) — keeps everyone aligned, maintains a positive atmosphere, great to work with
- Effective client relationship management (1) — balances business expectations and delivery constraints, knows when to give wins and when to push back
- Fast delivery under pressure (1) — impressive output speed and quality during time-constrained discovery work

**Areas for improvement:**
- Decisiveness on timelines and sign-offs (1) — needs to push harder for clear client decisions before moving forward, especially with indecisive clients
- Internal alignment before client meetings (1) — design work should be reviewed internally before presenting to avoid live clarification on calls

## What Went Well

### From Andrew

Joseph is very good at communication. He keeps everyone aligned and maintains a positive, collaborative atmosphere in the team. That makes a big difference day to day.

Great at client relationship. He knows how to balance business expectations and delivery constraints, give the client wins when it makes sense, and push back when needed.

### From James Totty

Within the inSepo discovery project, Joseph impressed me with his delivery speed. I believe the volume and quality of what we produced in that timeframe may have caught the client off guard (although you probably know more than me on that front). While leveraging AI as much as possible was part of the brief, it still required discipline to execute well. Joseph and I established a solid workflow to keep our documents in sync and deliver a comprehensive solution. He was a strong collaborator under a tight deadline and a joy to work with. He knew how to guide and help me when I was having to handle 7-8 interviews a week and code challenges on top of the solution architecture.

## What Could Be Better

### From Andrew

More decisive with timelines and locking decisions with the client. On insepo requirements were swinging (a very slippery client), and in this context, pushing harder for clear sign-offs before moving forward would’ve helped reduce some of the back and forth and ambiguity.

Alignment on design before key client meetings. I already shared this directly with Joseph. When we present work, we need to be fully aligned on our side first. He needs to review the design before is presented to allow for internal discussion and adjustments. There were a few moments where design decisions were being reviewed/clarified live on the call (between us), and that can make our work feel less solid. With a client like Insepo, who already struggled to make decisions, that kind of uncertainty can amplify things even more. A bit more upfront review together would make those conversations stronger and more confident for us.
