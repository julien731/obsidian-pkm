# 360 Review: Jui (2025)

## Summary

**Strengths:**
- Approachable and responsive (4 reviewers) — quick to help, unblocks the team, provides helpful answers when reached out to
- Well-structured stories and QA (2) — clear acceptance criteria, developer-friendly task breakdown, effective bug catching
- Proactive problem-solving (2) — finds ways around blockers, actively improves team processes (e.g., demo branch strategy)
- Positive team presence (2) — constructive, upbeat, diminishes stressful conversations and keeps the team focused

**Areas for improvement:**
- Ticket clarity and completeness (2) — some stories lack sufficient detail, leading to extra discussions during development
- Design update tracking (2) — UI changes from designers not always communicated to devs with corresponding tasks, causing rework
- Cross-team alignment (1) — stakeholders not always in sync on priorities; design changes sometimes discovered late by developers
- Technical understanding gaps (1) — deepening familiarity with core technical concepts would reduce back-and-forth
- Communication tone under pressure (1) — some statements come across as commands; managing tone during tight deadlines
- Self-verification before asking the team (1) — some checks (e.g., PR ordering) could be done independently

## What Could Be Better

### From Manh

- There were moments where gaps in technical understanding led to friction — for example, when concepts like "etag" came up, he needs explanation from the dev side to understand this concept clearly. Deepening familiarity with core technical concepts would help Jui communicate more effectively and reduce back-and-forth.
- At one point, the mobile team was assigned to update the email theme for a brand — a task that falls outside mobile's scope entirely. Improving understanding of what each team owns would prevent misrouted tasks, save time, and reduce frustration across teams.
- There have been instances where the dev team raised issues or concerns in the channel and responses came a bit later. Quicker acknowledgment — even a simple reaction or short reply — would reassure the team that their concerns are being seen and acted on.
