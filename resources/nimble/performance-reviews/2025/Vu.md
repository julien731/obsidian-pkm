# 360 Review: Vu (2025)

## Summary

**Strengths:**
- Clear, professional communication (4 reviewers) — combines firmness with respect across internal and external stakeholders, sets the standard for PM communication
- Leadership through uncertainty (3) — creates clarity in ambiguous situations, aligns teams on core objectives before execution
- Evidence-based execution (3) — builds POCs to validate assumptions, shifts debates from opinion to evidence, documents flows into repeatable practices
- Team development and support (3) — provides detailed, timely feedback focused on teaching frameworks rather than just fixing individual tasks
- Responsiveness (3) — quick on Slack, proactive alignment, action-oriented

**Areas for improvement:**
- Feedback skews toward gaps over strengths (1) — frequent improvement-focused feedback may reduce confidence over time; more explicit recognition could help
- Expectation clarity varies by context (1) — standards for depth vs. speed, escalation vs. independence aren't always explicit, making it hard to judge the right balance
- Onboarding structure (1) — information transfer for new PMs felt chaotic; a more structured process with feedback checkpoints would help
- Responsiveness on Jira comments and QA timing (1) — could be faster on ticket comments and earlier on QA verification

## What Went Well

### From Aiden

1️⃣ Leadership

He provides strong leadership during uncertainty and major transitions.

For example, when projects were suddenly paused or direction shifted, he communicated the reasoning clearly and helped the team refocus quickly instead of letting uncertainty drag on. When starting large initiatives like Loyalty or JB VN — where information was scattered and ambiguous — he simplified the problem space and aligned the team on the core objective before execution began.

This ability to create clarity in messy situations increases team stability and confidence.

2️⃣Execution

He drives execution through logic and validation rather than authority.

In cases where internal debates stalled progress, he built quick POCs to validate assumptions instead of continuing theoretical discussions. This shifted the team from opinion-based arguments to evidence-based decisions. (ref: https://nimble-co.slack.com/archives/C05PYU3HB51/p1752725286555249)

In another situation, while feasibility was still under research, he proactively validated technical constraints early. This allowed the team to make firmer decisions and push back on unrealistic client expectations with confidence. (ref: https://nimble-co.slack.com/archives/C05PYU3HB51/p1768326795987519?thread_ts=1768300390.518639&cid=C05PYU3HB51)

He also documented flows afterward, turning one-off actions into repeatable practices.

3️⃣ Communication

He communicates with clarity and professionalism across internal and external stakeholders.

When production misalignments occurred or features were discussed externally without prior alignment, he handled the conversations respectfully while clearly defining what was feasible and what was not. This prevented escalation while maintaining strong boundaries.

His communication style combines firmness with respect, which sets a strong example for PMs.

Ref: https://nimble-co.slack.com/archives/C05PSBYFY6S/p1770346354797779

4️⃣ Team Support & Development

He invests heavily in developing team capability.

His feedback is detailed and timely. For example, instead of simply pointing out missing acceptance criteria, he explains the thinking framework behind well-structured stories, helping improve long-term product thinking rather than just fixing individual tasks.

This approach accelerates learning and raises the quality bar of the team.

## What Could Be Better

### From Aiden

1️⃣ Feedback Balance

While frequent and detailed improvement feedback is valuable and has helped raise the quality bar, it often focuses more on gaps than on strengths or progress. Over time, this may create pressure and unintentionally reduce confidence.

Adding more explicit recognition of positive impact and growth could help balance performance expectations with motivation and reinforce what is working well.

2️⃣ Space for Divergent Perspectives

Because of his strong expertise and confidence, his opinions naturally carry significant weight in discussions.

In gray areas where multiple viewpoints may be valid, others may align quickly even if they still have open questions. Creating more deliberate space for alternative perspectives before converging on a decision could further strengthen ownership and encourage deeper exploration of ideas.

3️⃣ Expectation Clarity

Across different situations, the expectations given are reasonable and context-driven. However, the underlying standards are not always explicit.

At times, early escalation is expected; in other situations, more independent refinement is preferred. Similarly, sometimes higher detail is encouraged, while in other cases speed and assumption are prioritized.

While each case makes sense individually, clearer signals on what is expected in a given context would make it easier to consistently judge the right balance between depth, speed, ownership, and alignment.

More clarity on what “good enough” looks like in different scenarios could strengthen consistency and confidence in execution.
