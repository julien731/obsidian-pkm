# Lead UX/UI Designer JD

# What does the ideal candidate look like?

## Technical Knowledge

- You master the tools of your craft such as Figma, Sketch, Adobe CC, etc.
- You are intimately familiar all core steps of the design process: user research, user flows, wireframing, UI design.
- You have expert knowledge of user experience, design patterns, and platform-specific design (e.g., iOS vs. Android).
- You know how to create advanced, highly dynamic and convincing prototypes.
- You continuously stay up-to-date with industry trends, tools, and frameworks.
- You are familiar with project management; you have the ability and experience to lead design projects independently.

## People Management

- You have experience leading and mentoring other designers.
- You know how to identify your team members’ strengths, how to leverage those strengths, and how to develop them further.
- You know how to strengthen your team members’ weaknesses through mentoring and defining growth objectives for them.
- You have the ability to set objectives for the design team in order to reach the company’s higher goals.

## Organization

- You have excellent time management and work organization skills.
- You can ensure that any and all design work produced by the team meets the level of quality that is expected of the team.
- You can foster a learning environment amongst all the design team members.
- You have the experience to identify areas of improvement for the team, and you have the ownership to lead the documentation and implementation of those improvement.

## Communication

- **You have an English Professional working proficiency ([ILR level 3](https://www.icls.edu/foreign-language-programs/ilr-proficiency-levels/)).**
- You have great listening and cross-functional collaboration skills.
- You are used to working closely with Engineers to see through the correct implementation of your designs.

## Bonus

- You have some experience with development.

# What does a typical workday look like?

Although each day is usually different and presents its own challenges, there are common themes that you will face every day.

- Discovering new projects, understanding the pain-points and users expectations, defining how to approach the problems or innovations through user experience.
- Designing, wireframing, prototyping.
- Supporting Engineering with design implementation
- Interacting with Product Managers and iterating on designs.
- Interacting with external stakeholders, listening to and understanding their feedback while defending your design decisions.
- Reviewing your team’s work, both internal (assets produced for Nimble) and external (assets produced for clients).
- Mentoring your team members, providing support when necessary, figuring out how to help each and every one of them grow as UX/UI Designers.
- Reporting and providing insights to the Chief Product Officer.
- Participating in the definition of the Design team’s direction.

# Requirements

- 6+ years of experience as a UX/UI Designer.
- 2+ year of experience leading a team of designers.
