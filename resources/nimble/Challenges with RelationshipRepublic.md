# Challenges with RelationshipRepublic (RR)

RelationshipRepublic is a partner agency that Nimble collaborates with on client engagements (e.g. Singha, BTG). While the relationship has produced work, a number of recurring patterns create friction and risk that need to be actively managed.

## Structural Issues

- **They position Nimble as a dev shop, not a strategic partner.** RR sees themselves as the ones doing the "thinking" and expects Nimble to simply execute. This limits Nimble's ability to add value and weakens our positioning with the end client.

- **One-size-fits-all approach.** RR defaults to the one solution they know best (typically FMCG-adjacent) regardless of the client's actual problem. There's no genuine effort to tailor recommendations — they push what they know, not what fits.

## Commercial Concerns

- **Disingenuous commercial structure.** RR presents services to clients in separate, siloed proposals while simultaneously squeezing Nimble on price. In some cases, they've gone further — requesting a share of Nimble's own budget. This is a red flag for the long-term health of the partnership.

- **No strategic rigour on deals.** Their go-to-market approach is to force-feed FMCG knowledge into every pitch without a broader competitive or strategic framework. This is a liability when facing more sophisticated clients.

## Execution Risks

- **Chronic over-promising.** RR regularly adds scope, features, or commitments to client conversations without consulting Nimble first. This creates delivery risk and puts Nimble in a reactive position, managing expectations we had no part in setting.

## Notes

This is an evolving document — continue logging patterns as they emerge. These observations should inform how Nimble structures future agreements with RR: clear scope delineation, commercial transparency requirements, and a formal "no unilateral commitments" clause.
