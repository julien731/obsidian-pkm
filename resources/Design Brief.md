# Architectural Style

In general, we want a modern and minimalist design. We also want to try and blend indoor and outdoor spaces.

## Interior

We want the house design to follow our lifestyle and circulation flow. The house should optimize movement that matches the way we live. We do not want the house to force flows that don‚Äôt match our habits and routines.

Nature is also essential to us. We want to house to feel connected to nature.

The house needs to have storage space so as to keep it clean and clutter-free. A closed pantry area is probably something that we need.

### Workflows

Here is what a typical day looks like for us.

1. Wake up early.
2. Quick wash up.
3. Go out to walk the dog.
4. Get back home and do a physical workout.
5. Go for a swim.
6. Shower and get ready for the day.
7. Make tea/coffee and get work started (light work).
8. Go to the office/study for the work day.
9. Lunch break.
10. Back to work.
11. Finish work, go relax outside.
12. Prepare dinner and eat.
13. Relax, watch a movie or read books.

The house design should support this flow.

### No Go

As an example of what we **do not want,** our current house is laid out like so:

- Ground floor: kitchen and living room.
- First floor: master bedroom, bathroom.
- Second floor: guest bedroom, office, bathroom

Based on the workflow described above, our current house adds friction when going to the office area as it is on the last floor, while the master bedroom, where we only go once daily, is on the second floor.

## Exterior

We want a modern, minimalist exterior with strategically placed lighting for a pleasant night experience.

# Non-Functional Requirements

## Energy Efficiency

With the hot Thai weather, we want the house to be energy efficient to:

1. Keep the house cool
2. Minimize the electricity bills

We are not experts on how to achieve energy efficiency. There are three areas that we are considering:

- Using double-layer walls with heat insulation.
- Using double-glazing windows.
- Using geothermal energy (e.g., [Canadian Well](https://www.ecopassivehouses.com/canadian-wells/)).

[EcoHouseThailand.com](https://ecohousethailand.com/)

## Water & Filtration

- The water main must lead to the reserve tanks. No faucet, including outdoor, should be connected to the water main.
- There must be a filter before the water tanks (filtering the water from the main).
- All water distribution for inside **and** outside must be served by a water pump.

## Water Rooms

- Showers
    - ‚ÄúHidden‚Äù drainage hole
    - Enough length that we can step out of the rain shower flow for cleaning
    - Sloped design so that the water drains without the need for a lower floor height
- Bathtub with a view in the master bedroom (no other bathtubs)
