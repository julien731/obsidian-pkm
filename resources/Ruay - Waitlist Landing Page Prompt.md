Design a waitlist landing page for "Ruay", a personal finance app.
Use the design system already established in this project — colors,
typography, and component style should be consistent with the
existing screens.

FEELING
Calm and confident. This is a finance product — trust is the first
thing the page needs to earn. The tone is direct and empathetic,
like a friend who's good with money talking to someone who isn't.
Not flashy, not startup-hype. The whitespace should do as much
work as the copy. The single accent color carries all the urgency
— everything else is quiet.

LAYOUT
Mobile-first, single column, linear scroll. No navigation bar.
Max content width 640px, centered. Generous vertical padding
between sections (80px+). The page should feel like it takes
under 30 seconds to scan.

## Section 1 — Hero

Top: Brand name "Ruay" in heading font, centered, no logo.
Below: small pill/chip label — "Coming soon" or "Join the waitlist"
— in accent color, centered.

Headline (large, bold, centered, 2 lines):
"Know what you can
actually spend."

Subheadline (body, stone-600, centered, max 3 lines):
"Your bank says $3,000. But rent is due Tuesday, groceries come out
Friday, and your car insurance auto-pays next week. Ruay shows you
what's really yours."

CTA form (centered, stacked on mobile):
Email input field (full width, rounded-lg, placeholder
"your@email.com") + below it a full-width primary button
"Join the waitlist" in accent color.

Below CTA: avatar stack (3 overlapping small circular avatars)
+ text "Join 2,400+ people already waiting" in stone-500 small.
Leave this in but note it can be toggled off until real numbers exist.

## Section 2 — The Problem

Section label (small caps, accent color, centered):
"Sound familiar?"

Headline (centered, bold):
"Your bank balance is lying to you."

3 cards stacked vertically (full width on mobile). Each card:
slightly warm-gray background (stone-100), rounded-xl, 20px padding,
no border. Left-aligned text. No icons — copy carries the weight.

Card 1: "You see $3,000 — but $1,800 is already spoken for."
Card 2: "You budget in one app but spend from another."
Card 3: "You never know which account to actually use."

Each card headline in stone-900 medium weight, one sentence,
no subtext. The discomfort should land cleanly.

## Section 3 — The Solution

Section label (small caps, accent color, centered):
"How Ruay works"

Headline (centered, bold):
"Every envelope. Every dollar.
A real account."

3 value prop rows stacked (full width). Each row:
icon left (24px, accent color) + text block right.
Title in stone-900 medium + one-line description in stone-500.

Row 1 icon: envelope or tag
"Every dollar has a job —
Zero-based budgeting means nothing is left unassigned."

Row 2 icon: bank/building or link
"Envelopes tied to real accounts —
Always know where your money physically lives."

Row 3 icon: lock or shield
"Private by default —
Share with a partner or family member only when you choose."

Thin 1px stone-200 divider between each row. No cards here —
open, airy, list-like.

## Section 4 — Founding Member CTA

Full-width block, accent color background (warm, not harsh),
white text. Rounded-2xl. 40px padding.

Headline (white, bold, centered):
"Be a Founding Member"

Body (white-80, centered, small):
"The first 500 members get Ruay for $59/year — locked in forever."

Progress bar (optional): thin bar showing "312 of 500 spots claimed"
in white-40 track, white fill. Small label below.

CTA form (same pattern as hero):
Email input (white background, stone-900 text) +
full-width button below ("Claim your spot") in stone-900
background / white text — inverted from hero button.

## Section 5 — Footer

Minimal. Centered. Stone-400 text.

"Ruay" in heading font, centered, stone-700.
One line below: social icon links — X, Instagram, Facebook —
spaced evenly, stone-400.
Bottom line: "Made in Bangkok · 2026"
